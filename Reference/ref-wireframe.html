04 -- FAQ (Individual)

Common questions

What markets does Signal cover?
Signal covers 600+ world events markets across geopolitics, elections, policy, and macroeconomic triggers. We prioritize markets on Polymarket and Kalshi where thinly traded conditions create the most mispricing opportunity.
How is Q's forecast different from the market odds?
Market odds reflect crowd consensus, which is often driven by sentiment, recency bias, and low liquidity. Q's forecast is built from a structured evidence pipeline calibrated by human forecasters with verified track records. The gap between the two is the signal.
How often is the signal updated?
Signal updates daily. Markets with breaking developments may update intraday. You'll receive email alerts if a spread changes materially on a market you're watching.
What's included in the free tier?
The free tier includes 2 live markets in the Signal dashboard, weekly Substack digest, mobile app access (Farcaster and World mini apps), and access to the forecaster leaderboard. No credit card required.
Is this financial advice?
Quotient provides market intelligence, not financial advice. Signal is a research tool. All trading decisions are your own.

06 -- FAQ (Platforms)

Platform questions

How does the integration work technically?
Quotient provides a REST API with endpoints per market, per signal, and per intelligence package. You call our API and render the data in your UI. We provide structured JSON with all fields documented. Integration typically takes 1–2 engineering days for a basic implementation.
Does this affect our existing market mechanics?
No. Quotient's signal is a read-only data layer. It doesn't interact with your order book, liquidity pool, or market resolution logic. It sits alongside your existing markets as context.
What markets are covered?
600+ world events markets with active coverage on Polymarket and Kalshi. New markets are added weekly. You can request coverage for specific markets not yet in our database.
How is pricing structured for platforms?
Platform pricing is based on API call volume and market coverage scope. We discuss this during the pilot scoping call. No public pricing — reach out to get a number.
Can we white-label the signal?
Yes, with attribution requirements. The intelligence can be presented under your brand with a "Powered by Quotient" or "Verified by Quotient" mark. Exact co-branding terms are discussed during pilot.

05 -- FAQ (Developers)

Developer questions

What's the rate limit?
Default rate limit is 100 requests/minute. Volume tiers are available for agents making high-frequency calls. Contact us or configure via the developer dashboard.
How does x402 payment work?
x402 is a micropayment protocol for HTTP APIs. Your agent sends a payment header with each request. No API key management required — the payment is the authentication. We support USDC on Base.
How fresh is the data?
Core signal data updates daily. Breaking news triggers intraday updates on affected markets. The updated_at field in each response tells you exactly when that market's signal was last refreshed.
Can I access historical data?
Yes. The /v1/intelligence endpoint includes historical forecast data and resolved market track records. Useful for backtesting and building calibration models.
Is there a sandbox environment?
Yes. sandbox.quotient.social mirrors production with a fixed dataset. Free to use. No payment required. API key provisioned instantly on signup.

01 -- Mission
About Quotient

Prediction markets are cited like fact.
They shouldn't be.

World events prediction markets are thinly traded, easily moved, and systematically cited as authoritative by major media outlets. Quotient exists to fix that — by building the analytical layer these markets are missing.

The problem

A market with $40k in liquidity can set the headline odds on whether there will be a ceasefire in Ukraine. Major outlets cite that number as if it reflects expert consensus. It doesn't. It reflects who happened to trade that day.

What Quotient does

Bloomberg made equities legible through data aggregation and backward-looking analysis. Prediction markets are forward-looking and analytically underserved. Quotient is the verification layer that makes them trustworthy enough to act on.

02 -- How Q works

The accountability architecture

Most AI forecasting optimizes for accuracy through compute. Quotient optimizes for accuracy through accountability. The difference compounds.

Sources
Ranked by track record
1,600+ global sources weighted by historical accuracy on the types of events they cover. Recency bias is controlled. Source credibility compounds over time.
Forecasters
Verified, public records
Human forecasters contribute judgment with public track records. Accuracy earns influence. Poor performance reduces weight. No anonymous opinion — only auditable calls.
Q
Citable reasoning
Q's calls are grounded in specific sources and forecaster input. When Q is wrong, the failure is auditable. That accountability is what makes the signal trustworthy.
Bloomberg analogy: Bloomberg made equities legible through backward-looking data. Prediction markets are forward-looking and analytically underserved. Quotient fills that gap.
03 -- Research foundation

Built on a body of evidence

The methodology behind Q draws on decades of forecasting research and geopolitical intelligence work.
